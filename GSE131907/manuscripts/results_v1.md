# Results

## Cellular composition changes across NSCLC progression

Compared with normal lung tissue, primary tumors and brain metastases showed major alterations in cellular composition. Brain metastases were enriched in epithelial cells and displayed reduced immune infiltration.

## Progressive enrichment of exhausted CD8+ T cells and Tregs

The proportion of cytotoxic CD8+ T cells decreased from 28.4% in normal lung to 2.9% in primary tumors and 1.2% in brain metastases.

In contrast, exhausted CD8+ T cells increased from 2.5% to 13.1% and 15.6%, respectively.

Regulatory T cells increased from 2.7% in normal lung to 12.0% in primary tumors and 14.9% in brain metastases.

## Macrophage remodeling during metastatic progression

Alveolar macrophages dominated normal lung tissue (73.2%) but were progressively replaced by monocyte-derived macrophages.

Monocyte-derived macrophages increased from 4.8% in normal lung to 53.2% in primary tumors and 69.7% in brain metastases.
